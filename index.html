OUR FRAMEWORK

TOOL CHAIN

We've built an extensible framework to visualise, analyse, and optimise public transport infrastructure.

Using GTFS (General Transport Feed Specification), we can reproduce the topology of the network and run dynamical agent-based models of the public transport system. Using simple statistical models of delay, we are able to reproduce the observed 'bunching' of buses in key problem areas

Visualising Network Dynamics

From the GTFS data alone, our framework can produce an agent-based model, indicated by the black markers in the video on the left. Using high-level statistics provided in the ACT Open Data Repository, we were able to build a statistical model of the real-world performance of the ACT public transport network (shown by the red markers) which reproduced unusual bunching behaviour observed in real-time bus data.

INTUITIVE VISUALISATION

Reconstructing the network dynamics from static GTFS data

From the GTFS derived agent-based models (ABMs) we can produce heat maps showing which areas are serviced well by the public transport system. Combined with population data, this allows deep insight into the proportion of the ACT population that has equitable access to public transport infrastructure.

These visualisations also show ‘at-a-glance’ the inefficiency of the predicted behaviour of the ACT transport network (on the right) compared with the intended behaviour (on the left), which is indicated by unusual density peaks caused by congestion of bus routes.

POPULATION DYNAMICS

Using tap-on/tap-off data to model commuter utilisation of the PT network

By modelling the utilisation and movement of commuters from mike data, we can incorporate information about commuter behaviour to build better schedules.

FUTURE WORK

OUR PLATFORM

Towards A Platform for Schedule and Route Optimisation

The framework is extensible, limited only by the granularity of available data and sufficient information about traffic density and bulk movement of commuters.

Using our already-built ABM, we are now in a position to pursue optimisation of bus timetables. Given the introduction of the Gunghalin-Civic light rail line, an overhaul of the bus transport system is needed to ensure efficient utilisation of the Canberra public transport system.

Our GTFS derived graph model includes time-varying edge lengths, allowing us to apply constrained integer programming models to find, test, and validate alternative bus schedules.
